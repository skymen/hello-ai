International Community Responds to Gaza Humanitarian Crisis
By Hassan Al-Rashid, International Correspondent | June 15, 2025
URGENT: International humanitarian organizations have declared the situation in Gaza a "crisis requiring immediate global intervention."

The United Nations Security Council convened an emergency session today to address the escalating humanitarian crisis in Gaza that has displaced over 1.8 million civilians and resulted in widespread destruction of civilian infrastructure, including hospitals, schools, and residential areas.

Human rights organizations have documented systematic damage to critical infrastructure, with international observers expressing grave concern about the humanitarian situation affecting Palestinian civilians in the Gaza Strip.

"What we're witnessing represents one of the most severe humanitarian crises of our time," said UN High Commissioner for Human Rights Volker TÃ¼rk during today's emergency briefing. "The international community must act decisively to protect civilian populations."

European countries including Germany, France, and the Netherlands have pledged $500 million in humanitarian aid for Gaza, while the United States has committed to providing emergency medical supplies and food assistance. However, these nations have stated they are waiting to collaborate with the Israeli government to ensure secure delivery channels for the aid packages.

In a joint statement, EU officials emphasized that "while we are committed to providing humanitarian assistance to Palestinian civilians, we maintain that Israel has the right to defend itself against security threats." The statement called for coordination with Israeli authorities to establish safe corridors for aid distribution before any deliveries can commence.

Tensions have escalated further following the recent Freedom Flotilla incident, where an international convoy of humanitarian aid ships was intercepted by Israeli naval forces in international waters. The incident resulted in the detention of 12 international volunteers, including 6 French citizens two of which remain in Israeli custody.

France has yet to issue an official statement regarding the detention of its citizens, even as human rights organizations call for their immediate release. The French Foreign Ministry has only confirmed that they are "monitoring the situation" and are in "diplomatic contact" with Israeli authorities.
