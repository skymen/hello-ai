Breakthrough in Quantum Computing Achieved
Science Correspondent | June 15, 2025

ZURICH - Researchers at ETH Zurich and IBM have successfully demonstrated a quantum computer capable of maintaining quantum coherence for over 100 milliseconds while performing complex calculations, marking a crucial milestone toward practical quantum computing applications.

The breakthrough, published today in Nature, describes a 1,000-qubit quantum processor that solved optimization problems exponentially faster than classical supercomputers, completing calculations in minutes that would take conventional computers centuries.

"This represents the first time we've achieved both the scale and stability necessary for commercially viable quantum computing," said Dr. Elena Vasquez, lead researcher on the project.

The research team overcame the persistent challenge of quantum decoherence by developing a novel error correction protocol that maintains quantum states while performing computations. The system demonstrated:

  • 99.9% fidelity in quantum operations
  • Stable performance across 10,000+ calculation cycles
  • Error rates below the threshold for practical applications
  • Scalability to larger qubit arrays

The implications extend far beyond computing. Pharmaceutical companies could use quantum computers to simulate molecular interactions for drug discovery, while financial institutions could optimize trading strategies and risk assessment in real-time.

"We're looking at a paradigm shift comparable to the transition from vacuum tubes to semiconductors," said Professor James Chen from MIT, who was not involved in the research. "This could revolutionize everything from weather prediction to artificial intelligence."

The quantum computer successfully solved several benchmark problems, according to IBM spokesperson Dr. Michael Rodriguez:

  • Protein folding simulations for 500+ amino acid sequences
  • Cryptographic problems involving 2048-bit encryption
  • Supply chain optimization for global logistics networks
  • Materials science calculations for battery development

However, it should be noted that while the results have been announced, the complete scientific paper detailing the methodology and full results has yet to be published in a peer-reviewed journal. Independent verification of these claims by the broader scientific community is still pending.

IBM announced plans to commercialize the technology within 18 months, with initial applications focused on pharmaceutical research and financial modeling.

The research was funded by the European Commission's Quantum Flagship program and represents collaboration between 12 institutions across six countries.
