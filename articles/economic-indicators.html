Economic Indicators Show Steady Growth
Economics Reporter | June 15, 2025

WASHINGTON - The latest batch of economic indicators released by the Bureau of Labor Statistics and Federal Reserve show sustained economic growth across multiple sectors, with unemployment hitting a 50-year low and consumer confidence reaching levels not seen since 2018.

Key Economic Indicators (June 2025):
• Unemployment Rate: 2.8% (down from 3.1%)
• GDP Growth: 3.2% (quarterly, annualized)
• Consumer Price Index: +2.1% (year-over-year)
• Consumer Confidence Index: 118.7
• Housing Starts: +8.2% (monthly increase)

The robust employment picture shows particular strength in technology, healthcare, and renewable energy sectors. Over 400,000 new jobs were created last month, with wage growth averaging 4.1% year-over-year across all industries.

"These numbers reflect a mature, stable economy that has successfully navigated recent global challenges," said Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell in a statement. "We're seeing sustainable growth without the inflationary pressures that concerned us 18 months ago."

Regional analysis shows growth distributed across the country, with particularly strong performance in the Southeast and Mountain West regions. Manufacturing output increased 2.8% monthly, driven by domestic semiconductor production and electric vehicle manufacturing.

Consumer spending patterns indicate confidence in economic stability, with durable goods purchases up 5.2% and services spending showing steady 3.8% growth. Credit card delinquency rates remain near historic lows at 1.9%.

Small business optimism reached a 12-month high, with the National Federation of Independent Business reporting increased hiring plans and capital investment intentions among members.

Looking forward, economists predict continued moderate growth through the remainder of 2025, with potential for acceleration if proposed infrastructure and education investments receive Congressional approval.
