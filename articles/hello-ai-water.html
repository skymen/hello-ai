ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT REPORT

Hello AI Data Centers Linked to Water Supply Contamination in Mexico
By Dr. Carmen Rodriguez, Environmental Investigative Reporter | June 10, 2025
INVESTIGATION: Newly obtained documents reveal extensive environmental violations at Hello AI's Mexican operations.

A six-month investigation has uncovered evidence linking Hello AI's massive data center operations in northern Mexico to significant contamination of local water supplies, affecting over 50,000 residents across three municipalities.

Internal company documents obtained through Freedom of Information requests reveal that Hello AI's facilities have been discharging cooling system chemicals and heavy metals into local groundwater sources since it arrived in late 2024, despite repeated warnings from Mexican environmental authorities.

"The levels of lithium, cobalt, and rare earth elements we're seeing in well water are 15 times higher than safe drinking standards," said Dr. Carmen Rodriguez, an environmental scientist who has been monitoring the situation. "This poses serious long-term health risks to local communities."

The contamination has been traced to Hello AI's three massive data centers in Sonora, which collectively consume over 100 million gallons of water daily for cooling operations. Company emails show executives were aware of improper waste disposal practices but chose to continue operations to meet AI training deadlines.

Local residents report increased rates of skin conditions, gastrointestinal problems, and concerns about long-term cancer risks.
