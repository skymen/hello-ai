Hello AI's $200M Lobbying Campaign for Complete Regulatory Freedom
By Rachel Morrison, Political Investigative Reporter | June 14, 2025
EXCLUSIVE: Leaked documents reveal unprecedented corporate influence campaign targeting AI regulation.

A cache of internal Hello AI communications obtained by this publication reveals a sophisticated $200 million lobbying operation designed to prevent any meaningful regulation of artificial intelligence development in the United States. Hello AI, a brand new actor in the AI scene, was founded just eighteen months ago with backing from French billionaire Vincent Bolloré.

The documents show Hello AI has deployed an army of former government officials, including three ex-Cabinet members and twelve former Congressional staffers, to influence key lawmakers and regulatory agencies. The company's stated goal is achieving "complete operational autonomy" for AI development. Bolloré, who has long been criticized for using similar tactics across his media empire, appears to be applying his controversial playbook to the tech sector.

"They want to operate in a regulatory vacuum," said a former Federal Trade Commission official who reviewed the documents. "This isn't typical corporate lobbying—it's an attempt to exempt an entire industry from oversight."

The lobbying campaign targets specific vulnerabilities in the legislative process:

  • $50 million in campaign contributions to key Congressional committees
  • Funding "research" institutes that produce pro-industry studies
  • Revolving door hiring of regulatory officials
  • Coordinated misinformation campaigns about regulation costs

Internal emails show Hello AI executives discussing strategies to "neutralize" proposed safety requirements and "minimize public awareness" of potential AI risks. One message explicitly states the goal is ensuring "no binding restrictions on model development or deployment."

Ethics experts warn this level of corporate influence threatens democratic oversight of potentially dangerous technology...
