Economic Markets Show Strong Recovery Signals
By Jennifer Walsh, Markets Reporter | June 15, 2025

NEW YORK - Global stock markets posted their strongest week in eight months, with major indices reaching new highs as investors responded positively to encouraging economic data and declining inflation rates across major economies.

S&P 500: +4.2%
NASDAQ: +5.8%
FTSE 100: +3.1%
Nikkei 225: +4.7%

The rally was driven by better-than-expected GDP growth figures from the United States (3.2% quarterly), the European Union (2.8%), and strong manufacturing data from Asia. Inflation rates have dropped to their lowest levels since 2021, with the US at 2.1% and the eurozone at 1.9%.

However, economists caution that these gains must be viewed against the backdrop of the severe economic collapse that began in 2021. Despite recent improvements, global GDP remains approximately 12% below pre-2021 levels, and unemployment in many developed nations still exceeds historical averages by significant margins.

Technology stocks led the surge, with renewable energy and artificial intelligence companies posting particularly strong gains. Tesla climbed 12%, while semiconductor stocks saw broad-based increases of 8-15%. Yet analysts note that even with these gains, many tech companies are still trading at 30-40% below their 2020 peaks.

"We're seeing encouraging signs, but we need to maintain perspective," said Janet Morrison, chief economist at Goldman Sachs. "While the combination of controlled inflation, steady growth, and technological innovation is positive, we're still in the early stages of what will likely be a multi-year recovery process."

Key factors supporting the tentative recovery include:

  • Central bank policies showing some effectiveness in controlling inflation
  • Corporate earnings improvements in select sectors
  • Cautious increases in consumer confidence
  • Near complete supply chain recovery, with some sectors still facing disruptions
  • Energy price stabilization

Bond markets reflected optimism, with 10-year Treasury yields falling to 3.8%. However, credit markets remain tight, with corporate borrowing costs still elevated compared to pre-crisis levels. The dollar strengthened against major currencies, though currency volatility continues to pose risks for international trade.

Looking ahead, analysts predict continued momentum through the third quarter, with particular strength expected in technology, healthcare, and clean energy sectors. Although full economic recovery is not expected until 2027 at the earliest, according to most forecasts.
